Introduction

In 2010, the GDP of emerging economies of the G20 represented 33% of the total GDP of all G20 countries. By the end of the decade, in 2018, emerging economies contributed with 39% of the total GDP of the G20 (which represented 66.5 trillion USD). China's economic growth was responsible for most of this change: in 2018, China's GDP mounted to 13.6 trillion USD. Nonetheless, other emerging economies have grew abruptly between 2010 and 2018, as is the case of India, South Korea (with an economic growth of 49% and 35% respectively), as well as Saudi Arabia and Indonesia (with an economic growth of 17%).

Overall, the economic breach between emerging and developed economies showed a reduction during the 2010s. For this reason, we will take a look at the major trends of the G20 economies with regards to expenditure on military, health, and education between 2010 and 2017. Our aim is not only to identify the main differences between emerging and developed economies, but also to understand which can be the main drivers of growth during this decade.
