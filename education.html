The data analysis performed here contributes to a better understanding of:

1. Education Expenditure per person in G20 countries

  • It can be seen that Australia is the country which has been on top in terms of Education expenditure, with the highest value of 3564 US dollars per person in 2010.
  • Followed by France, Canada, Germany, Japan and US with an average expenditure of 2300 US dollars per capita.
  • High population can be a big reason for India and China to have lowest Education Expenditure.

2. Comparison of Educational spending to that of country's GDP

  • Overall, the education expenditure as a percentage of GDP has decreased among the G20 countries.
  • It can be seen that education expenditure is no longer a priority for the advanced countries, whereas emerging countries like Indonesia and India has seen a significant rise in education expenditure.

3. Comparison of the per person educational spending to the per person GDP

  • It can be clearly seen that a very less percentage of the country's total spending is spent on Education in almost all the countries.
  • Australia, Japan, Canada, Germany, France do show considerable spending.

4. Average Expenditure on Eduaction vs Military Expenditure

  • In most of the countries, average expenditure on Education is more than double to that of Military Spending.
  • Whereas, in Russia and US, the spending is uniformly distributed in case of education and Military.
